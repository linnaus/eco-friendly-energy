About Biogas

Biogas is a clean, renewable fuel produced from the anaerobic digestion of organic waste like cow dung and kitchen scraps. It reduces waste, cuts greenhouse emissions, and provides affordable energy for households.

How to Build a Digester

Materials Needed

25L plastic gallon, PVC pipes, tyre tube, gas valve, glue gun, silicon seal, epoxy, kitchen waste, and cow dung.

Step 1

Prepare feedstock: chop kitchen waste, mix with cow dung in ratios like 1:1, 1:2, or 1:3 with water.

Step 2

Assemble digester using airtight container, connect PVC/gas hose to tyre tube for gas storage, and seal tightly.

Step 3

Store digester at room temperature (24–30°C). Monitor gas daily using water displacement or storage tube expansion.

FAQs

Q: Is biogas safe?
A: Yes, if digesters are sealed properly and gas is handled carefully.

Q: How much gas can I get?
A: From your project data: up to 34 L/kg feedstock (best at 1:3 cow dung:kitchen waste).
